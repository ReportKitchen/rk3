Design Principles for Teacher Preparation

Enacting the science of learning and development

Linda Darling-Hammond, Maria E. Hyler, and Steve Wojcikiewicz, with Joy Rushing
Learning Policy Institute & EdPrepLab · July 2025

Over the last several decades, we have learned a great deal about how people learn and develop — from neuroscience, the developmental and learning sciences, and fields like anthropology and social psychology.

B I

This report identifies design principles for teacher preparation, built on recent syntheses of that research and on the wisdom of practice found in leading-edge programs.

It is written for the people who design preparation: program faculty, accreditors, and licensing agencies.

Five principles for preparation

  • Curriculum rooted in a deep understanding of learners and development
  • Skills, habits, and mindsets of an equitable educator
  • Rich, experiential learning opportunities
  • Pedagogical alignment and modeling
  • Supportive relationships in communities of practice

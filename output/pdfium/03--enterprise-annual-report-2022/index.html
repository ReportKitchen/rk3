Annual Report from Enterprise Community Partners

For 40 years, Enterprise Community Partners has been devoted to a single mission: to make home a place of pride, power and belonging. And with your support, this year we broke records and broke barriers like never before.

Enterprise is unique in the marketplace. We are the only national nonprofit that addresses America’s affordable housing crisis from every angle. We take pride in building inclusive communities by increasing the housing supply, promoting upward mobility and resilience, and advancing racial equity through scalable and sustainable solutions, programmatic work, advocacy and investment strategies.

In a country where for many, the prospect of an affordable home in a safe community is a distant dream, Enterprise Community Partners is committed to dismantling the enduring legacy of systemic racism and poverty.

We don’t do it alone. Every day, your support fuels our mission and work across the country. We have planted deep roots. Partnership is in our DNA, and we’ve forged relationships with the changemakers on the ground who are making a real, tangible impact.

As we assume leadership roles on the Board, we see and feel the power of Enterprise – and we hope you feel the same sense of power in the pages ahead. Thank you for your support.

To the next 40 years!

Rick and Phyllis

Breaking the Cycle of Housing Instability in Detroit

Together with the Gilbert Family Foundation, ProMedica and DTE Energy, we launched the Detroit Home Repair Fund, a $20 million program designed to help build capacity for non-profit partners in order to provide critical home repairs to low-income homeowners.

Nearly 8,500 owner-occupied homes in Detroit are defined as “inadequate or severely inadequate,” by the Department of Housing and Urban Development’s American Housing Survey. This instability creates a negative cycle that erodes the health of families and threatens the preservation of generational wealth.

The Detroit Home Repair Fund was designed to complement existing programs offered by the city to holistically address housing stability. Partners of the fund oversee all facets of residential home repairs, from soliciting bids from contractors to conducting post-job inspections. By providing homeowners with necessary improvements, the fund will help improve the overall baseline of Detroit housing.

In addition to stabilizing housing, the program will provide necessary interior health and exterior safety upgrades and raise home values while building homeowner equity. It plays a critical role in our ongoing commitment to improve neighborhood opportunity, sustainability and resilience.

Hosting Alaska’s First Housing Innovation Summit

We partnered with the Association of Alaska Housing Authorities to bring together 200+ participants from across Alaska and the nation to strategize innovations to address Alaska’s unique affordable housing challenges. The two-day summit saw partnership as a major theme, with organizations calling for culturally and climate-responsible solutions.

Alaska’s current housing stock is overcrowded, energy inefficient and lacking in affordable options. According to a 2018 Alaska Housing Finance Corporation report, overall home construction needs to increase by 90 percent to meet the state’s needs.

The summit explored existing innovations and partnerships as well as new solutions that can potentially increase housing development. Attendees were encouraged to make connections and provided access to useful tools designed to help them advance solutions that would best fit their individual communities.

Putting Preservation First

Keeping rental properties affordable for the long-term, especially for low-income families and communities of color, is a proven strategy to prevent displacement and keep people in their homes.

In the fall of 2022, we launched the nation’s first preservation academy to catalyze and support housing stability efforts. Designed as a regional resource, we launched in California as a series of no-cost, virtual trainings and peer-learning sessions.

The academy provides developers, affordable housing providers and operators, and public sector practitioners with the information needed to preserve unsubsidized and currently affordable homes across California.

In addition to the virtual training, our academy includes a select cohort of developers and housing providers committed to preserving affordable homes throughout the state.

We are providing this group with additional resources, catalytic grants, and technical assistance funds to enhance their ability to preserve the critical small-to-medium multifamily housing stock.

While the California Preservation Academy was our first, it was designed to be replicated across the country. We’ve used the regional academy model to share best practices across the country with successful programs like the Rural Preservation and Resilience Academies.

The current academy will be enhanced and adapted for other areas in the coming months, starting with Colorado.

Bringing Faith-based Development Nationwide

Earlier this year, we gathered with Wells Fargo in Atlanta’s first United Methodist Church to announce the nationwide expansion of our Faith-based Development Initiative.

Houses of worship control tens of thousands of acres of land, often in high-value metro areas where rents are skyrocketing and the demand for more affordable housing is acute. Rarely do these faith leaders have the resources or technical expertise to turn that land into safe, affordable and healthy homes.

Since 2006, our Faith-based Development Initiative has given partners across the Mid-Atlantic the tools they need to make informed decisions as they navigate the development process.

Our technical and financial resources have
helped dozens of churches create 1,500 affordable homes, turning their unused properties into engines of opportunity and prosperity.

Now, with $8.5 million in support from the Wells Fargo Foundation, we’re ready to go national. As we expand the program nationwide, we’ll be looking at a total of 6,000 new, affordable homes in communities that are in desperate need of investment.

We look forward to continued collaboration with houses of worship and the development industry partners who will work with them to make our shared vision a reality.

The Covid-19 pandemic underscored how much the experience our residents have in our communities matters. Through resident-led councils, satisfaction surveys, and one-on-one meetings, we listened to what they need to thrive and launched Resident Voices.

In addition to being a platform for residents to share their stories, Resident Voices was designed to encourage residents to be part of the community-building process by creating pathways to share feedback, input and guidance. Record Investment Expedites Lead Safe Progress

In 2022, the Lead Safe Cleveland Coalition’s Lead Safe Home Fund, managed by Enterprise, experienced unprecedented investment, with a $52.5 million pledge made by the Cleveland Clinic, a $13 million pledge from the City of Cleveland via the American Rescue Plan Act and a $1 million federal earmark.

Childhood lead poisoning has reached crisis levels in the United States and the number of children poisoned in Cleveland Ohio is about four times the national average. Lead-based paint and lead dust are the most common sources of exposure in Cleveland, making this a public health crisis with a housing solution.

The Lead Safe Cleveland Coalition is a publicprivate partnership convened and staffed by Enterprise to address lead poisoning. The cornerstone of the work is supporting the City of Cleveland’s Lead Safe Certification law which requires residential rental properties built before 1978 to be proactively certified as lead safe.

Our approach to lead poisoning is preventive, comprehensive, and long-term and incorporates proven low-cost, scalable solutions. Given this new influx of capital, we are able fulfill our comprehensive vision far sooner than expected. In broad strokes, expanded programming will encompass additional workforce development, additional grants to property owners, lead safe childcare, and relocation assistance for families facing displacement.

Doubling Down on a Proven Program

To scale needed housing solutions, the Wells Fargo Foundation selected Enterprise to lead a $20 million Housing Affordability Breakthrough Challenge. In 2020, the inaugural program awarded $2.5 million in grants to six organizations to support wide-ranging innovations in housing construction, finance and resident support and services.

In fall of 2022, the National Building Museum opened a major exhibit featuring the program and innovation winners. Open through fall 2023, A Better Way Home explores the program’s breakthrough ideas, the organizations leading them, and the people and communities impacted.

Fast Company magazine highlighted A Better Way Home and provided an in-depth review of the Housing Affordability Breakthrough Challenge winners, calling each a “replicable idea that can make more and better housing.”

During a celebration of the program in December 2022, president of the Wells Fargo Foundation, Otis Rolley, announced that Wells Fargo would expand its commitment to fund a second $20 million competition in collaboration with Enterprise.

# Guidance · FOIA BASICS FOR ACTIVISTS
`02--foia-basics-for-activists-may-2019` · ~18pp · ~8073 words · intro section ~833 words

## What we read
- **What it is:** A practical how-to guide from the Center for Constitutional Rights on using FOIA and state open records laws to support activism and organizing.
- **Audience:** Activists, organizers, and social movements — plus lawyers who support them.
- **Core message:** Anyone can use FOIA to pry loose government records for a campaign — but it's slow, so plan your strategy, write your request carefully, and be ready to appeal or litigate.

## Stats found (5)
- **300,000+ pages** — NDLON v. ICE ultimately produced over 300,000 pages of information on the Secure Communities program.
- **3+ years** — The NDLON v. ICE litigation took over three years to complete.
- **20 business days** — FOIA requires federal agencies to determine and notify a requester of their response within 20 business days.
- **90 days** — You have 90 days to appeal any determination made by a federal agency.
- **9 exemptions** — Federal agencies can invoke nine statutory exemptions to redact or withhold documents.

## Stories & case studies found (6 · 0 personal, 6 case study)
- _strongest_ · `caseStudy` · **NDLON v. ICE (Secure Communities campaign)** — The National Day Laborer Organizing Network used FOIA to expose how ICE's Secure Communities program worked, winning 300,000+ pages after 3+ years of litigation and a landmark ruling from Judge Shira Scheindlin.
- _solid_ · `caseStudy` · **St. Charles Parish Sheriff records request (Dakota Access Pipeline)** — CCR filed a narrow 2017 state records request about the sheriff's trip to observe DAPL protests, received some documents within weeks, then litigated for more over the following year.
- _solid_ · `caseStudy` · **Color of Change surveillance request** — CCR and Color of Change filed a FOIA about DHS/FBI surveillance of Movement for Black Lives activists, deliberately telling a story in the request itself as an advocacy tool.
- _thin_ · `caseStudy` · **Detention Watch Network — 'Banking on Detention' report** — Using information produced by their FOIA request, DWN and CCR released the 2015 report 'Banking on Detention.'
- _thin_ · `caseStudy` · **Herman Ferguson records requests** — CCR's early-1990s public records requests supporting activist Herman Ferguson revealed the extent to which the FBI and NYPD were surveilling him and his organization.
- _thin_ · `caseStudy` · **Louisiana Bucket Brigade / Bayou Bridge Pipeline** — Director Anne Rolfes described how her group and grassroots partners filed a state records request seeking the truth about a new pipeline threatening land, water, and health.

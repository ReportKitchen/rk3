## What we read
- **What it is:** A WRI research brief making the economic case for climate adaptation, summarizing a paper that analyzed 320 adaptation projects using the 'triple dividend' framework.
- **Audience:** Governments, development partners, and climate finance decision-makers who see adaptation as a cost rather than an investment.
- **Core message:** Adaptation isn't a financial burden — it's one of the smartest investments available, returning over $10.50 per $1 with benefits that accrue even when disasters never strike.

## Stats found (6)
- **$10.50+** — Benefits yielded over 10 years for every $1 invested in adaptation.
- **$359 billion** — The annual gap between funding needed to adapt to climate change and what's available.
- **$1.4 trillion** — Expected 10-year benefits from the analyzed investments, which cost over $133 billion.
- **27%** — Average return on individual adaptation investments analyzed.
- **78%+** — Average returns for health-sector adaptation investments — the highest of any sector.
- **65%+** — Share of monetized benefits unrelated to expected climate shocks — accruing even without disasters.

# Compared to our info-design-goals, we have significant gaps to close.

The design principles document surfaced these findings:

- 4 foundations: EdPrepLab's work on this project was supported by the Carnegie Corporation of New York, Skyline Foundation, Spencer Foundation, and W. Clement & Jessie V. Stone Foundation
- 4 external reviewers: reviewed the report, including experts from UC Berkeley, University of Wisconsin–Madison, and the American Institutes for Research

Those are meaningless in the context of this work.

RWJF health survey surfaced these findings:

- ±3.5 percentage points was the overall margin of error for the survey
- 97% of completed surveys were conducted via English web

These round to a value of meaningless.

Meanwhile there's an entire section called "Key Takeaways" with exactly the information we want: 
- Most New Jerseyans do not frequently feel personally discriminated against because...
- Nearly 3 in 10 residents (29%) believe Black people in New Jersey face...
- A majority of residents, 71%, believe some New Jerseyans have less of an opportunity....


Atlantic council:
- 16 recommendations private firms can consider as they take steps to support the region ( this tells us NOTHING interesting )
- 9 private-sector stakeholders and experts provided insights through one-on-one interviews ( Same ) 

Meanwhile Atlantic Council explicitly offers 5 key opportunities each with a summary and 3 listed bullets underneath.  It could not be MORE clear what the key findings of this report are.

Oxfam report isn't bad, except
- there's a box called "Box ES: Inequality in numbers" which lists precisely the bullet points we're after.  It's not in any summary or findings.
- there's an explicit top-level set of problem statements (Four ways that corporate power fuels inequality) and recommendations (Reining in corporate power: three practical steps) in the executive summary.  Those should be called out. 

## How do we fix this?

what if we tried looking for patterns like these, instead of specifically something called exec summary, highlights, findings, etc?  Some reports might have none but some might have exactly what we want in a label we'd never guess (Box ES: Inequality in numbers)

Som examples:

Gates earth:
````
Among the report’s key findings[..]are their contributions to building stronger communities, companies and countries.

For communities: Resilience investments could generate..
For companies: The global adaptation and resilience market could reach...
````

Within the executive summary section is a callout box:
````
"What is Resilience? At the core, resilience is the capacity.."
````
Identifying and handling callout boxes is a key part of RK3 and we need to leverage that -- we could offer a menu of them to pick from in assembling the landing page - either in full or summarized.  Some callouts are a half-page or more but some are very brief, like this one.
